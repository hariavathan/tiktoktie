Game Theory 101

Minimax Search

Minimax is a decision rule used in game theory for two-player zero-sum games. It works by recursively searching the game tree. The maximizing player (AI) tries to get the highest score, while the minimizing player (Human) tries to force the lowest score.

Alpha-Beta Pruning

It is an optimization technique for Minimax. It maintains two values, alpha (maximum score guaranteed to maximizer) and beta (minimum score guaranteed to minimizer). If alpha ≥ beta, the rest of the branch is pruned, as the opponent can force a better outcome elsewhere.

Is AI Unbeatable?

Yes, at the "Unbeatable" level. Since Tic-Tac-Toe has only 9! = 362,880 possible games (and far fewer unique states, exactly 5,478 legal positions), the AI searches the entire tree in a fraction of a millisecond. Under optimal play by both sides, the game will always end in a Draw.
